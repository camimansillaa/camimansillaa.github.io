Materials & Tools

Dorodango-making is about simplicity — transforming ordinary soil into a polished sphere. The table below lists the basic materials and their purposes.

Material Reference Table

A breakdown of materials and their uses in the Hikaru Dorodango process.
Category Item Purpose Notes
Core Materials Soil / Clay Forms the base of the dorodango sphere. Choose soil with a fine grain texture for best results.
Water Binds soil particles; used for shaping. Use sparingly — too much moisture can cause cracking.
Polishing Tools Fine Dust Used to create smoother layers in later stages. Collected from sieved dry soil.
Polishing Cloth For final shine and reflection. Microfiber or soft cotton preferred.
Optional Enhancements Natural Pigments Add subtle color variations. Mix with dry soil during the last layer.

Quick Preparation Checklist

  • Gather materials in a clean workspace.
  • Sieve your soil to remove stones or debris.
  • Prepare a soft towel for drying the dorodango.

Pro Tips (Nested List Example)

  1. During shaping:
    • Keep your hands slightly damp to prevent sticking.
    • Use small circular motions to form even surfaces.
  2. During polishing:
    • Switch to finer dust as you progress.
    • Buff with gentle pressure — patience gives the best shine.
